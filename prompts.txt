The writing style of the New York Times can be described as formal and objective writing style, with a focus on reporting the facts and opinions of those involved in the story, clear and concise, and usage of direct quotes. The vocabulary used is generally formal and sophisticated. Some technical terms are also used as well as some colloquial phrases. The publication appears to be written for a general audience.
